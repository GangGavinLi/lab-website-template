Lab Achieves 'Triple-Double' Research Milestone

Gang Li Principal Investigator Mississippi State University
December 15, 2025
awards recognition milestone

We’re excited to share that our research group has reached a major milestone that we’re calling our “Triple-Double” achievement - significant growth across funding, students, and research output!

The Gang Li Research Lab at Mississippi State University has achieved remarkable growth in its second year, with major federal funding awards, expanded graduate student team, and breakthrough publications in AI-enabled energy systems.

What is the Triple-Double?

In basketball, a triple-double means achieving double digits in three statistical categories in a single game. For our lab, we’ve achieved our own version:

  1. Double-digit funding growth - Securing over $4M in new grants
  2. Doubled research team size - Expanding from 2 to 7+ Ph.D. students
  3. Double-digit publications - Publishing 10+ papers in high-impact journals

Major Funding Achievements

This year brought unprecedented funding success:

  • NSF NRT Grant ($3M): Co-PI on “Smart Agriculture Energy Innovation Network” building workforce for sustainable rural energy communities
  • DOE TEAMER Program ($171K): PI on reliability analysis of infinitely variable transmissions for tidal energy converters
  • NSF EPSCoR Grant ($300K): PI on predictive condition monitoring for distributed wind energy using blockchain technology
  • NSF PFI-TT Grant ($550K): Co-PI on novel IVT technology for tidal current energy harvesting

Research Team Expansion

Our team has grown significantly with talented new Ph.D. students:

  • William Mayfield - Working on federated learning frameworks for distributed wind systems
  • Youssef En-Nali - Joining to work on AI-enabled predictive maintenance
  • Eli Herrington - Focusing on renewable energy integration
  • Sara Mouafik - Advancing AI forecasting for energy systems
  • Doha Bounaim - Developing time-series forecasting models
  • Obed Kamulegeya - Contributing to smart infrastructure research
  • Zaki Moutassem - Completing dissertation on physics-informed deep learning

Research Breakthroughs

Our expanded team has produced impressive research output:

AI-Powered Energy Forecasting

Published groundbreaking work on multivariate CNN-Bi-LSTM models for wind-to-hydrogen production forecasting in the International Journal of Hydrogen Energy.

Physics-Informed Deep Learning

Zaki Moutassem’s research on CNN-LSTM architectures enhanced with physical domain knowledge was featured in Algorithms, advancing predictive maintenance for underground power cables.

Federated Learning for Wind Systems

Developed privacy-preserving frameworks for distributed wind system monitoring, presented at ASME ES2025.

Advanced Drivetrain Technologies

Continued innovation in infinitely variable transmissions, with new analytical models published in Mechanism and Machine Theory.

Recognition and Awards

This growth has been recognized by the university and broader community:

  • 2024 Junior Faculty Outstanding Research Award from MSU College of Engineering
  • NSF I-Corps Best Accomplishment Award for technology commercialization
  • DOE EnergyTech UP Faculty Explorer Award for energy innovation

Looking Forward

With this strong foundation, we’re positioned to make an even greater impact in 2026:

  • Expanding collaborations with national laboratories and industry partners
  • Launching new research initiatives in hydrogen production and storage
  • Growing our team with additional talented graduate students
  • Advancing technology transfer through our Transfinity IVT commercialization

This achievement represents the collective effort of our entire team, our funding agencies (NSF, DOE), and our university. We’re grateful for the support that makes this research possible and excited about the innovations ahead.
